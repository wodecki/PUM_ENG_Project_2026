# MajsterPlus — Business Scenario

## The Company

**MajsterPlus** is a mid-sized Polish DIY (Do-It-Yourself) retail chain specializing in home improvement products. Founded in 2015, the company operates **12 stores** across Poland's major cities: Warszawa, Kraków, Łódź, Wrocław, Poznań, Gdańsk, Szczecin, Bydgoszcz, Lublin, Białystok, Katowice, and Gdynia.

MajsterPlus serves a broad customer base — from professional contractors and serious DIY enthusiasts to occasional weekend renovators. The product range spans 8 categories: Tools, Paint, Plumbing, Electrical, Garden, Flooring, Building Materials, and Lighting.

## The Problem

Customer acquisition costs in the Polish retail market have been rising steadily. MajsterPlus's VP of Marketing, Katarzyna Nowak, has noticed a concerning trend: **too many customers make one or two purchases and never return**. The marketing team's current approach — sending identical promotional newsletters to all customers — is expensive and increasingly ineffective.

A preliminary analysis suggests that approximately **18% of MajsterPlus customers are "lapsed"** — they have not made a purchase in the last 90 days. This represents a significant revenue risk.

## Your Role

You have been hired as a **data analyst** to help MajsterPlus solve this problem. Your mandate:

1. **Understand the customer base** — Who are MajsterPlus's customers? What patterns distinguish active customers from lapsed ones?

2. **Predict customer lapse** — Build a machine learning model that identifies which customers are at risk of lapsing, so the marketing team can intervene proactively.

3. **Recommend a reactivation strategy** — Translate your model's predictions into a concrete business recommendation: Which customers should receive a targeted reactivation campaign? What is the expected financial impact?

## The Data

MajsterPlus has provided you with two datasets:

- **`customers.csv`** (~5,000 records) — Customer demographics, purchasing behavior, and engagement metrics. Includes a binary target variable `is_lapsed` (1 = no purchase in last 90 days, 0 = active).

- **`transactions.csv`** (~25,000 records) — Individual transaction records linked to customers, including dates, amounts, product categories, and payment methods.

The data was extracted from MajsterPlus's systems on **January 15, 2025**. It covers customer registrations from January 2022 through December 2024.

> **Important**: Like all real-world data, these datasets contain quality issues — missing values, inconsistent formats, data entry errors, and columns that require type conversion before analysis. Part of your job is to identify and handle these issues.

## Business Context for the Reactivation Campaign

MajsterPlus is considering a **targeted reactivation campaign** for customers predicted to be at risk of lapsing. The proposed approach:

- **Intervention**: Send a personalized promotional voucher (50 PLN value) via email and SMS to at-risk customers, combined with a phone call from the customer service team.
- **Campaign cost per customer**: ~80 PLN (voucher value + operational costs for outreach)
- **Expected outcome if successful**: The reactivated customer returns to their historical purchasing pattern for the next 12 months.

The key business question: **Is it more profitable to target only the high-risk customers identified by your model, or to contact everyone?** Your model should help MajsterPlus maximize the return on their reactivation investment.

## The Five Mini-Projects

Your analysis follows the **CRISP-DM** (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) methodology, broken into five sequential mini-projects:

| MP | Title | CRISP-DM Phase | Focus |
|----|-------|---------------|-------|
| MP1 | Business Context & Data Exploration | Business Understanding + Data Understanding | Load data, explore distributions, identify quality issues, train a "sneak peek" baseline model |
| MP2 | Data Cleaning & Feature Engineering | Data Preparation | Handle missing values, remove outliers, convert types, encode categoricals, engineer new features |
| MP3 | Baseline Modeling & Algorithm Comparison | Modeling | Train LogisticRegression and RandomForest, compare via confusion matrix, precision, recall, ROC-AUC |
| MP4 | Model Evaluation & Business Impact | Evaluation | Construct retail cost matrix, calculate profit per record, optimize classification threshold |
| MP5 | Model Comparison & Final Recommendation | Evaluation (synthesis) | Compare 3–4 models across statistical performance, business profit, interpretability, and fairness |

Each mini-project builds on the previous one. If you fall behind, golden baseline checkpoints are provided so you can start any mini-project from a known state.
