# PUM: Designing Services Using AI Methods — ML Mini-Projects

## The MajsterPlus Challenge

You are a **data analyst** hired by **MajsterPlus**, a mid-sized Polish DIY retail chain operating 12 stores across Poland. Approximately **18% of customers are "lapsed"** — they haven't purchased in over 90 days. Your job: build a machine learning model to predict which customers are at risk, and recommend a targeted reactivation campaign.

Over five mini-projects, you'll follow the **CRISP-DM** methodology from raw data to a business recommendation:

```
MP1 → MP2 → MP3 → MP4 → MP5
 │      │      │      │      │
 ▼      ▼      ▼      ▼      ▼
Data   Data   Model  Business Final
Under- Prep   Build  Evaluate Recommend
stand
```

## The Five Mini-Projects

| MP | Title | CRISP-DM Phase | What You Do |
|----|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **MP1** | Business Context & Data Exploration | Business + Data Understanding | Load data, explore distributions, find quality issues, train a baseline model |
| **MP2** | Data Cleaning & Feature Engineering | Data Preparation | Parse Polish dates, clean currency strings, handle missing values, remove outliers, encode categoricals, scale features |
| **MP3** | Baseline Modeling & Algorithm Comparison | Modeling | Train Logistic Regression and Random Forest, evaluate with confusion matrix, ROC curves, feature importance |
| **MP4** | Model Evaluation & Business Impact | Evaluation | Build a cost matrix, calculate campaign profit, optimize the classification threshold, perform lift analysis |
| **MP5** | Model Comparison & Final Recommendation | Evaluation (synthesis) | Compare 3-4 models on statistics, profit, fairness, and interpretability; write a business recommendation |

## Your Starting Point

Your baseline model (MP1) achieves **~0.83 ROC-AUC** with just 6 raw features. By MP5, you'll have cleaned the data, engineered features, and compared multiple models to find the one that maximizes business profit — and you'll discover that the "best" statistical model isn't always the most profitable one.

## Assessment Model

- **5 mini-projects × 10 MCQ questions = 50 questions total**
- Each test is delivered via Edux with a **48-hour window** and **3 attempts**
- Questions 1-5: conceptual (theory, methodology, interpretation)
- Questions 6-10: project-specific (interpretation and reasoning about your notebook results)
- Between attempts you see your score (e.g., "7/10") but NOT which questions you got wrong
- **Before starting each test**, make sure you have computed all key values in your notebook

## Reproducibility Requirements

All students use the **same dataset** and **same random seed**:
- **Seed**: 42 (set at the top of every notebook)
- **Libraries**: scikit-learn 1.4-1.5, pandas 2.x, numpy <2.0
- **Preprocessing**: Follow the exact 12-step order in MP2

This means everyone should get the **same numerical results** — which is how the MCQs work.

## Getting Help

- Read `2. data/data_dictionary.md` before every mini-project
- Read the MP brief (`1. project/mpN_brief.md`) for step-by-step guidance
- If your numbers don't match the checkpoint, check your preprocessing order
- Use `3. notebooks/scripts/verify_checkpoint.py` to compare your MP2 output against the golden baseline
